What This Is

The deal is simple.

A nonprofit land trust buys the land and holds it forever. Twelve households each contribute $12,500, build their own dwellings, farm together, govern together, and live sovereign lives on shared ground. Nobody gets rich. Nobody gets evicted. The land can never be sold for profit.

You Never Own the Land

The trust does. You hold a 99-year ground lease. You own your home, your time, and your freedom. No bank can foreclose. No landlord can raise your rent. No market crash can take your home.

You Farm Together

Regenerative organic. Cover crops, compost, rotational grazing, biodiversity-first design. Inspired by Apricot Lane Farms and Joe Hollis. The land heals because you're on it, not in spite of it.

You Govern Together

One household, one vote. Council roles with term limits. Total financial transparency. An external ombudsperson. And a matriarchal framework that asks one question first: who is vulnerable, and what have we built to protect them?

You're Free to Leave

Any time, for any reason. You take your equity, your belongings, and nobody shames you. A community you're afraid to leave isn't a community. It's a trap.

Core Values

Three rules. Everything else flows from these.

1

Children's safety is prioritized in every decision. Full stop.

Background checks on every adult. Cameras in shared spaces. Two-adult rule for all children's activities. An external ombudsperson children can talk to. Zero tolerance for abuse. This is the non-negotiable that governs everything else.

2

Be who you are.

Your body. Your mind. Your identity. Your beliefs. Your neurotype. Your capacity. Every part of you is welcome here, not just the parts that are convenient. Modified duty for different abilities. Multiple ways to participate in governance. No conformity pressure. No loyalty tests.

3

Don't harm yourself, others, the community, or the land.

Regenerative farming practices protect the soil. Safeguarding protocols protect the people. Conflict resolution protects the relationships. Financial transparency protects the trust. The community that harms nothing is the community that sustains everything.

The Path

How it actually works.

Not a theory. A step-by-step process with checklists, templates, financial models, and governance documents — all open-source, all tested.

1

Find Your People

Recruit twelve households who share the vision. Screen carefully — wrong people cost more than waiting. Trial visits, honest conversations, background checks. Everyone reads the Hard Truths before they commit.

2

Form the Trust & Secure Land

Incorporate the CLT, pool your buy-ins ($12,500 each), find the right land. Test the water, test the soil, verify the zoning. Close with the trust holding title. Nobody's name is on the deed — everybody's future is.

3

Build the Infrastructure

Water, septic, power, roads, fencing, common house. Temporary housing first, permanent dwellings phased in. Cameras installed. Emergency binder in every home. Accessibility built in from day one — not retrofitted.

4

Start Growing

First gardens, first chickens, first compost. Begin the three-year organic transition. Plant the fruit trees that won't produce for years — that's faith in the future. Establish your CSA, your farmers market presence, your value-added products.

5

Live the Culture

New Moon and Full Moon dinners. Solstice celebrations. Skill shares. Work parties. Game nights around the fire. A duty rotation so everyone gets three weeks off. A governance model that protects dissent. A matriarchy that builds the floor so no one falls through.

Radical Honesty

The hard truths.

If we only told you the beautiful parts, we'd be lying. Here's what you need to know before you fall in love with the idea.

You never own the land.

The trust does. You hold a ground lease. If you need to build equity through real estate speculation, this isn't your path.

Privacy is different here.

Your neighbors will know when you're struggling. There are cameras in shared spaces. Community is intimate by design.

Rural isolation is real.

You will be far from hospitals, concerts, and diverse restaurants. The nearest trauma center might be an hour away.

The money is tight.

Realistic household income range: $10,000 – $20,000/year from farm sources. You need low overhead and creative income streams.

There are cameras.

In all shared outdoor spaces. For child safety, accountability, and property security. Your home is private. The commons are monitored.

There are gun rules.

Locked storage. Separate ammo. No firearms in common areas. Annual certification. Children's lives are the one thing we refuse to put to a vote.

How We Stay Honest

Built so it can't become the thing you're afraid of.

Every intentional community that failed started with good values. We've studied what went wrong and built structural safeguards into the foundation.

No One Is Above the Rules

Including founders. The covenant applies to household one the same as household twelve.

Term Limits on Leadership

Three years max. Then step aside. No one becomes the permanent anything.

Total Financial Transparency

Every member sees every dollar. Dual signatures. Quarterly reports. Annual third-party audit.

Right to Leave

Any time, any reason, no penalty. Take your equity. No shunning. No guilt.

External Ombudsperson

An independent professional who visits annually, meets with anyone (including children), and can contact authorities directly.

Annual Self-Assessment

The community checks itself against the ICSA coercive community checklist every year. Uncomfortable. Essential.

Protection of Dissent

You can vote no. You can raise concerns. Whistleblower protection is in the covenant.

Freedom of Association

Talk to anyone. Maintain outside relationships. Children have full social lives beyond the property. No information control. Ever.
